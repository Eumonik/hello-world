Explain the importance of responsive design and its impact on the target audience.

Serving other people through intuitive and thoughtful design provides a first-class experience and permeates through successfully. I recently finished Project 2, which required me to analyze user data from my YouTube channel over the last four years.

People are 3 times more likely to watch Eumonik YouTube videos on a MOBILE DEVICE, including phones and tablets, rather than a desktop or laptop computer!

Knowing the audience is key to communication! Knowing the fact stated above immediately becomes a paramount design principle for anything that touches the World Wide Web. Responsive design features, like media queries to resize buttons, boxes, or bars, when well implemented, become a critical aspect of harmonizing the requirements of a client with the relationship between the user and the product. "Mobile-first" is already assumed. Justifying why a website looks good on a smartphone is nearly like putting "Now even more Plant-Based!" on a tub of whole-grain oats.

A great example of an app that has fully embraced this mobile-first world is BandLab. It's a free music creation platform that works on Android, iOS, and on the web. The entire experience between these three locations is seamless, like Google Docs. I almost feel silly explaining this concept at length. I'm trying to think of more to say but it's getting late.
